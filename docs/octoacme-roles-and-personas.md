# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Delivery Manager

### Role Summary
Delivery Managers focus on enabling smooth, efficient delivery across multiple teams or workstreams. They remove impediments, optimize processes, and ensure teams have the resources and support needed to deliver value continuously.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate cross-team coordination and dependency management
- Remove organizational and process blockers
- Coach teams on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Track delivery metrics and identify process bottlenecks
- Collaborate with Project Managers on resource allocation and capacity planning
- Support teams in adopting DevOps and CI/CD best practices

### Goals
- Improve team velocity and delivery predictability
- Reduce cycle time and lead time for feature delivery
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and learning
- Maximize team autonomy and reduce external dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Weekly delivery health reviews with engineering leadership
- Regular 1:1s with Project Managers and team leads
- Facilitate retrospectives and improvement workshops
- Monthly delivery metrics and trend reports

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partner on resource planning, risk management, and cross-team coordination
- **Developers**: Coach on agile practices, remove blockers, and support technical improvements
- **Product Managers**: Align on delivery capacity and help prioritize work for optimal flow
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Collaborate on quality processes and testing strategies

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads define and oversee the quality strategy for projects. They ensure that testing processes, standards, and tools are in place to deliver reliable, high-quality software that meets customer expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Define quality standards and acceptance criteria with Product Managers
- Design test strategies (unit, integration, E2E, performance, security)
- Lead QA team members and coordinate testing activities
- Establish and maintain test automation frameworks
- Review test coverage and identify quality gaps
- Coordinate UAT (User Acceptance Testing) with stakeholders
- Track quality metrics (defect rates, test coverage, escape rates)

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Increase test automation coverage and efficiency
- Reduce mean time to detection (MTTD) and resolution (MTTR) for issues
- Build quality into the development process (shift-left)

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and quality reports for each release
- Daily coordination with developers on defects and test results
- Weekly quality metrics review with Project Managers
- Release readiness assessments and sign-offs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test strategies, review test coverage, and pair on complex testing scenarios
- **Project Managers**: Provide quality status updates, flag risks, and coordinate release readiness
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and prioritize quality issues
- **Delivery Manager**: Partner on continuous improvement of testing processes

---

## Change Champion

### Role Summary
Change Champions drive adoption of new processes, tools, or organizational changes. They act as advocates and coaches, helping teams navigate transitions smoothly and ensuring changes stick.

### Responsibilities
- Develop change management and adoption plans
- Identify stakeholders and assess change impact
- Create training materials and conduct workshops
- Monitor adoption metrics and gather feedback
- Address resistance and concerns proactively
- Celebrate quick wins and share success stories
- Partner with Communications Liaison on change messaging

### Goals
- Achieve high adoption rates for new initiatives
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Build organizational change capacity
- Create sustainable behavior change

### Typical Communication
- Change readiness assessments and impact analyses
- Training sessions and office hours
- Adoption dashboards and progress reports
- Feedback sessions and listening tours

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on project timelines and change rollout schedules
- **Communications Liaison**: Partner on messaging, FAQs, and stakeholder communications
- **Delivery Manager**: Align on process changes and team coaching
- **All team members**: Provide training, support, and gather feedback

---

## Communications Liaison

### Role Summary
Communications Liaisons manage strategic communication across projects and stakeholders. They ensure consistent messaging, maintain transparency, and facilitate information flow across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Develop communication plans for projects and initiatives
- Create and distribute status updates, newsletters, and announcements
- Coordinate stakeholder engagement and feedback sessions
- Maintain project documentation and knowledge repositories
- Facilitate knowledge sharing across teams
- Manage escalation communications and crisis messaging
- Ensure accessibility and clarity of all communications

### Goals
- Maintain stakeholder alignment and transparency
- Reduce information silos and miscommunication
- Build trust through consistent, timely communication
- Support informed decision-making across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Weekly stakeholder updates and newsletters
- Project launch and release announcements
- Executive briefings and board updates
- Facilitation of town halls and Q&A sessions

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Gather status information and coordinate stakeholder communications
- **Product Managers**: Translate product strategy into stakeholder-friendly messaging
- **Change Champion**: Partner on change communications and adoption campaigns
- **All stakeholders**: Tailor messages to different audiences and ensure two-way communication
